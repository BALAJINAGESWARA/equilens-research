💬 Management Commentary (from CEO Carol B. Tomé & CFO Brian Dykes, MD&A)

Strategic Direction: Management is executing a "Customer First, People Led and Innovation Driven" strategy targeting growth in healthcare, small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs), and international. Deliberate volume reduction from the largest customer (expected >50% reduction by June 2026 vs. 2024) is being offset by a shift to higher-yielding volume.

Revenue Quality: Revenue per piece in U.S. Domestic increased 6.5%, driven by an average 5.9% net increase in base and accessorial rates implemented throughout 2025, favorable customer/product mix, and higher fuel surcharges. Management cited this as "several consecutive quarters of product and customer mix improvements."

Cost Environment: Operating expenses increased due to: (1) USPS outsourcing transition costs and excess staffing for Ground Saver product; (2) higher third-party lease expense from aircraft capacity constraints following Q4 2025 MD-11 fleet retirement; (3) higher workers' compensation; (4) weather-related costs. These were partially offset by headcount reductions and labor savings from volume decline.

Transformation Program: Network Reconfiguration & Efficiency Reimagined achieved approximately $600 million in program cost savings in Q1 2026. Full-year 2026 expected savings of ~$3 billion YoY. The Driver Choice Program (voluntary separation for full-time U.S. drivers) will generate ~$1.2 billion in separation costs, mostly in Q2 2026.

Tariff Situation: UPS has begun filing refund requests for ~$500 million in IEEPA tariffs paid. No amounts recognized as of March 31, 2026. Ongoing monitoring — material developments could emerge in subsequent quarters.

International Strength: Investments in Incheon hub expansion, new Taiwan logistics center, and European ground speed improvements are intended to support premium volume growth. AHG (healthcare) acquisition from Q4 2025 contributing to logistics revenue.

4. Bull & Bear Points

🟢 Bull Case

  • ▲Revenue quality improving: U.S. Domestic revenue per piece +6.5% YoY to $13.91, driven by 5.9% rate increases and favorable customer/product mix shift. International rev/piece up 10.7% to $22.49.
  • ▲SCS turnaround impressive: Supply Chain Solutions operating profit surged from $46M to $205M YoY (+345.7%), with margin expanding from 1.7% to 8.1%. Healthcare logistics (AHG) contributed $188M in incremental logistics revenue.
  • ▲International revenue growing: International Package revenue up +3.8% to $4.54B, driven by export revenue per piece +9.1% and strong domestic per-piece growth of +15.9% in EMEA/Americas.
  • ▲$3B annualized savings target: Network Reconfiguration program delivered ~$600M savings in Q1 alone, on track for ~$3B full-year savings. 23 buildings closed, 27 more identified — structural cost base shrinking.
  • ▲Strong liquidity: $5.8B in cash and equivalents as of March 31, 2026. Operating cash flow of $2.22B in the quarter. Maintains access to $3B in revolving credit facilities.
  • ▲IEEPA tariff refund optionality: UPS has filed for ~$500M in tariff refunds under the Supreme Court IEEPA ruling. Not yet recognized — upside if recovered in future periods.

🔴 Bear Case

  • ▼Volume collapse in U.S. Domestic: Average daily package volume fell 8.0% YoY to 16,040K — driven by deliberate exit from largest customer (Amazon) and e-commerce pruning. Ground volume down 7.9%, Air down 9.0%.
  • ▼Operating margin compression severe: U.S. Domestic operating margin collapsed from 6.8% to 3.6% (−320 bps), with operating profit down 47.4%. Even non-GAAP adjusted margin of 4.0% signals structural pressure.
  • ▼USPS outsourcing transition costly: Ground Saver outsourcing to USPS drove facility/transportation costs up $341M and created excess operational staffing. Benefits expected only later in 2026.
  • ▼Driver Choice Program to hurt Q2: ~$1.2B in separation costs expected primarily in Q2 2026. This will significantly impact reported earnings in the next quarter — investors should expect a noisy quarter.
  • ▼Debt load rising: Total debt at $24.4B vs. $24.1B at year-end 2025. Interest expense up +19.8% YoY to $266M. Finance lease obligations jumped from $781M to $1,085M due to new aircraft finance leases ($305M of new aircraft leases in Q1).
  • ▼Goodwill impairment risk in SCS: GFF and HLD reporting units within SCS (combined goodwill ~$1.6B) have limited excess fair value over carrying value. Macro deterioration or volume declines could trigger non-cash impairment charges.

5. Business Risks

⚡ Near-Term Risks (1–2 Quarters)

🚛
Q2 2026 earnings disruption: ~$1.2B in Driver Choice Program separation costs expected mostly in Q2, plus ongoing USPS outsourcing transition. Q2 GAAP earnings will likely show another significant decline.
✈️
Aircraft capacity constraints: Following MD-11 fleet retirement in Q4 2025, UPS is leasing third-party aircraft at higher cost. While three 767-300s were delivered in Q1, full normalization expected only in Q4 2026.
🌍
Middle East conflict & fuel: Higher jet fuel costs from Middle East conflict pressured fuel expense (+$25M YoY). If conflict escalates, further fuel cost headwinds are likely, as UPS uses weekly indexed pricing.
⚖️
Tariff policy uncertainty: ~$500M in IEEPA tariff refund requests are pending — outcome unclear. Additional phases of refund process may not cover all entry types. Customer tariff pass-through creates receivable risk.
🧾
Workers' compensation reserve deterioration: Workers' compensation expense increased $67M in Q1 due to unfavorable development in prior year claims. If claims continue to develop adversely, expense could persist.

🏗️ Structural / Long-Term Risks

📦
Amazon volume exit & mix shift: Volume from the largest customer (Amazon) is being deliberately cut >50% by June 2026. Revenue quality can partially offset, but total volume may remain structurally lower for several years.
🤝
Labor negotiations exposure: Pilot CBA with IPA is past amendable date (Sep 2025) — negotiations ongoing. Mechanic CBA (Teamsters Local 2727) amendable Nov 2026. Work stoppages or costly settlements could materially impact operations.
📉
SCS goodwill impairment risk: GFF (~$877M) and HLD (~$738M) reporting units have limited valuation headroom. Tariff changes, volume shifts, or macro deterioration could trigger impairments — both failed to meaningfully beat their carrying values at Jul 2025 test.
🏦
Pension obligations: Pension & postretirement obligations of $6.67B on balance sheet. Company expects to contribute an additional ~$1.2B through 2026. Rate sensitivity and demographic changes can increase obligations.
🌐
De minimis trade policy changes: International volumes, particularly China-to-U.S. and EMEA, were negatively impacted by de minimis exclusions and global trade policy shifts. Further tightening could reduce cross-border small-parcel volumes.
