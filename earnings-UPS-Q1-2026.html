4. Bull & Bear Points

🟢 Bull Points

  • ▲Revenue quality improving: U.S. Domestic revenue per piece +6.5% YoY to $13.91, driven by 5.9% net rate increases and favorable customer/product mix shift. International rev/piece up 10.7% to $22.49.
  • ▲SCS turnaround impressive: Supply Chain Solutions operating profit surged from $46M to $205M YoY (+345.7%), with margin expanding from 1.7% to 8.1%. AHG healthcare acquisition contributed $188M incremental logistics revenue.
  • ▲International revenue growing: International Package revenue +3.8% to $4.54B, driven by export revenue per piece +9.1% and domestic per-piece growth of +15.9% in EMEA/Americas.
  • ▲$3B annualized savings target on track: Network Reconfiguration delivered ~$600M savings in Q1 alone. 23 buildings closed, 27 more identified — structural cost base shrinking.
  • ▲Strong liquidity position: $5.8B cash and equivalents. Operating cash flow of $2.22B. $3B revolving credit facilities remain undrawn.
  • ▲IEEPA tariff refund optionality: ~$500M in refund requests filed. Not yet recognized — potential upside if recovered in future periods.

🔴 Bear Points

  • ▼Volume collapse in U.S. Domestic: Average daily package volume fell 8.0% YoY to 16,040K — driven by deliberate Amazon exit and e-commerce pruning. Ground volume −7.9%, Air −9.0%.
  • ▼Operating margin compression severe: U.S. Domestic operating margin collapsed from 6.8% to 3.6% (−320 bps). Non-GAAP adjusted margin of 4.0% still signals structural pressure.
  • ▼USPS outsourcing transition costly: Ground Saver outsourcing drove facility/transportation costs up $341M with excess operational staffing. Benefits expected only later in 2026.
  • ▼Q2 earnings disruption ahead: ~$1.2B in Driver Choice Program separation costs expected primarily in Q2 2026 — investors should expect a significantly impacted quarter.
  • ▼Debt load rising: Total debt $24.4B vs. $24.1B at year-end 2025. Interest expense up +19.8% YoY to $266M. Finance lease obligations jumped $304M from new aircraft leases.
  • ▼Goodwill impairment risk in SCS: GFF and HLD reporting units (combined goodwill ~$1.6B) have limited fair value headroom. Macro deterioration could trigger non-cash impairment charges.

5. Business Risks

⚡ Near-Term Risks (1–2 Quarters)

🔴
Q2 2026 earnings disruption: ~$1.2B Driver Choice Program separation costs expected mostly in Q2, plus ongoing USPS outsourcing transition. Q2 GAAP earnings will likely show another significant decline.
🔴
Aircraft capacity constraints: Following MD-11 fleet retirement in Q4 2025, UPS is leasing third-party aircraft at higher cost. Full normalization expected only in Q4 2026.
🔴
Middle East conflict & fuel costs: Higher jet fuel from Middle East conflict pressured fuel expense +$25M YoY. Further escalation could create additional fuel cost headwinds.
🟡
Tariff policy uncertainty: ~$500M IEEPA tariff refund requests pending. Additional refund phases may not cover all entry types. Customer tariff pass-throughs create receivable risk.
🟡
Workers' compensation reserve deterioration: Workers' compensation expense increased $67M in Q1 due to unfavorable development in prior year claims. Adverse development could persist.

🏗️ Structural / Long-Term Risks

🟡
Amazon volume exit & mix shift: Volume from largest customer cut >50% by June 2026. Revenue quality can partially offset, but total volume may remain structurally lower for several years.
🟡
Labor negotiations exposure: Pilot CBA with IPA past amendable date (Sep 2025). Mechanic CBA (Teamsters Local 2727) amendable Nov 2026. Work stoppages or costly settlements could materially impact operations.
🟡
SCS goodwill impairment risk: GFF (~$877M) and HLD (~$738M) reporting units have limited valuation headroom. Tariff changes or volume shifts could trigger non-cash impairments.
🟡
Pension obligations: Pension & postretirement obligations of $6.67B on balance sheet. Company expects to contribute an additional ~$1.2B through 2026. Rate sensitivity can increase obligations.
🟡
De minimis trade policy changes: International volumes negatively impacted by de minimis exclusions and global trade policy shifts. Further tightening could reduce cross-border small-parcel volumes.
