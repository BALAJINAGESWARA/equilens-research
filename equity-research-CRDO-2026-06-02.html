Credo Technology Group is a fabless semiconductor company providing high-speed, secure, and power-efficient connectivity solutions for AI data infrastructure. Founded in 2008 and publicly listed in January 2022, Credo has undergone a rapid transformation from a diversified connectivity supplier into a focused AI cluster interconnect pure-play.

The company's core product — the Active Electrical Cable (AEC) — uses Credo's proprietary SerDes (Serializer/Deserializer) technology to deliver copper-based interconnect with up to 1,000× greater reliability than conventional laser-based optical modules, at lower power consumption. This reliability advantage has driven rapid adoption by hyperscalers building massive AI training clusters where a single cable failure can halt a 100,000-GPU training run.

Credo's business model is fabless: it designs all products internally but outsources manufacturing to TSMC and other foundries, creating an asset-light, high-gross-margin model when scale is achieved. Revenue has tripled in FY2026 to $1.3B and management guides to another year of 80%+ growth in FY2027.

2. Business Analysis

Revenue Model

  • Product Revenue (97%): Chip and cable assembly sales to hyperscalers and cloud operators. Revenue is per-unit shipped × ASP. AEC ASPs are in the low-double-digit dollars per cable (100G); ZeroFlap Optics have three-digit ASPs (vs two-digit for discrete DSP components).
  • IP Licensing (3%): SerDes IP licensed to custom silicon designers. Recurring but declining as a percentage of total.
  • No subscription recurring revenue — pure semiconductor product company with typical hyperscaler order-driven revenue profile.

Product Lines

  • AEC: Core revenue driver (~85% of Q4 FY2026 revenue). 100G per lane dominant; 200G per lane emerging. Purple-branded "ZeroFlap" reliability is the key differentiator.
  • Optical ICs / DSPs: Retimers for optical transceivers; growing. Optical revenue doubled YoY in FY2026.
  • ZeroFlap Optics: Full optical module product — combines Credo SerDes with optics. Three-digit ASPs. FY2027 major ramp planned.
  • SerDes Chiplets / IP: Licensed to hyperscaler custom AI chip designers. Small but strategic.
  • PCIe Gen6 AEC: Extension of AEC to PCIe server connectivity. Production revenue beginning 2026.
  • DustPhotonics (acq. May 2026): Silicon photonics PICs. Vertical integration of optical manufacturing stack.

Competitive Moat Assessment

Moat Rating: Narrow (building toward Wide)

  • SerDes technology leadership: Credo's proprietary SerDes architecture delivers a rare combination of reach, latency, and power efficiency. This is the foundational IP across all products — AEC, optical DSPs, ZeroFlap Optics — creating a deep technical moat that is hard to replicate in 1-2 years.
  • AEC reliability advantage: 1,000× reliability vs commodity optical modules is a measurable, demonstrable claim — not marketing. For hyperscalers running 100,000+ GPU clusters where downtime costs millions per hour, this creates real switching costs: once qualified, incumbency is durable.
  • Hyperscaler co-development relationships: Custom AEC designs co-developed with hyperscaler engineering teams create sticky relationships. Switching requires re-qualification — a 6-18 month process that creates inertia favouring Credo.
  • Vertical integration progress: DustPhotonics acquisition adds PIC manufacturing capability — progressing toward owning SerDes + DSP + PIC + system integration in optical. Competitors cannot easily replicate this vertical stack.

Management Quality

  • Bill Brennan (CEO): Previously held senior roles at Cavium and QLogic. Track record of executing on product roadmap — every quarterly guidance beat by large margins. Has successfully managed the transition from diversified connectivity to AI-focused pure-play.
  • Capital allocation: DustPhotonics $750M acquisition is the largest commitment. Aggressive but strategically justified. Prior Hyperlume acquisition (FY2025) was smaller and additive.
  • Governance flag: 124 insider net sell transactions since March 2026 with zero purchases. CTO and other executives sold above the analyst consensus price target. This is a yellow flag worth monitoring.

Revenue trajectory analysis: Credo went from $106M in FY2022 to $1.3B in FY2026 — a 12× increase in four fiscal years — driven entirely by AEC adoption in AI hyperscaler clusters. The FY2024 dip (-8%) reflected broader semiconductor inventory correction; the AEC business was growing even then.

Gross margin dynamics: Non-GAAP gross margins have expanded from 60.7% in FY2022 to ~66% in FY2026, reflecting the mix-shift to higher-value AEC and optical products. The Q3→Q4 decline from 68.6% to 64.5% is product mix (ZeroFlap optics and ALC ramp at lower initial margins). Recovery to 67–69% guided in Q1 FY2027 as scale improves optical margins.

Operating leverage is real: Non-GAAP EPS grew from $0.09 in FY2022 to an estimated ~$3.36 in FY2026 — a ~37× increase in EPS on a 12× revenue increase. R&D as a percentage of revenue has declined from ~45% to ~22% as the top line has scaled, creating substantial operating leverage.

7. Bull vs Bear

Bull Case

  • ✦AEC structural dominance in AI clusters: 1,000× reliability advantage is measurable and real — not marketing. As GPU cluster sizes grow to 100,000+, the operational cost of link failures makes Credo's AEC the only rational choice. This is a durable competitive position that takes years to erode.
  • ✦FY2027 guided >80% revenue growth on a $1.3B base: Achieving $2.3B+ in FY2027 would make Credo one of the fastest-growing semiconductor companies at this scale. The combination of 200G AEC ramp + optical portfolio inflection + fifth hyperscaler revenue provides multiple growth levers simultaneously.
  • ✦Optical inflection in H2 FY2027 — structural TAM expansion: ZeroFlap optics, silicon photonics PICs (DustPhotonics), and optical DSPs transform Credo from an AEC copper company to a full AI interconnect platform company — a dramatically larger TAM and a higher-multiple business model.
  • ✦PEG 0.7x is inexpensive on growth-adjusted basis: At ~56x NTM P/E on 80%+ revenue growth, Credo is cheaper on PEG than MRVL (1.4x), ALAB (1.6x), and most growth semiconductor peers. The -14% post-earnings reset creates a more attractive entry than pre-earnings levels.
  • ✦Four consecutive quarters of large EPS beats: Beat range +13.75% to +44.20% with average +31.6%. The consistency of execution gives credibility to management guidance — when Credo guides, it typically beats materially.

Bear Case

  • ✦Hyperscaler concentration is existential near-term risk: Three customers representing an estimated 70–80% of revenue. Any AI capex pause, cluster upgrade delay, or AEC dual-sourcing decision from a top-3 customer would materially impair FY2027 revenue with no short-term offset from smaller customers.
  • ✦H2 FY2027 optical inflection is entirely unproven: DustPhotonics (closed May 27) must be integrated, ZeroFlap optics must ramp in volume, and silicon photonics PICs must achieve yield and reliability targets — all within 6 months. Any of these failing delays the most important investment catalyst.
  • ✦Insider selling acceleration at elevated prices: 124 net sell transactions, zero purchases since March 2026. The CTO sold above the analyst consensus target price. Insiders have better visibility into the H2 optical ramp. This level of net selling immediately before a catalyst is a meaningful yellow flag.
  • ✦Gross margin ceiling in mid-60% range: Long-term non-GAAP gross margin framework is guided ~mid-60% — below NVIDIA (78%), Marvell (59%), and Broadcom (65% optical). The AEC model shares economics with hyperscaler customers; significant margin expansion beyond 68% is structurally limited.
  • ✦Broadcom and Marvell competitive pressure: Both have larger R&D budgets, broader product portfolios, and incumbent hyperscaler relationships. Dual-sourcing of AEC or optical components at even one hyperscaler would cap Credo's market share growth — this risk grows as the AEC market becomes strategically important enough to attract more investment from incumbents.

Key Risks

  • Execution: DustPhotonics silicon photonics yield and integration; ZeroFlap optics volume ramp; 200G per lane qualification timelines at hyperscalers.
  • Concentration: 70–80% revenue from 3 customers. Revenue cliff risk if any pauses or dual-sources.
  • Foundry: TSMC dependency for advanced node manufacturing (3nm for optical DSPs). Any TSMC supply disruption has outsized impact.
  • Valuation: Even at post-earnings ~56x NTM P/E, any guidance miss triggers significant multiple de-rating. CRDO fell -14% on a beat-and-raise quarter — the bar is high.
  • Dilution: ATM equity offering used to fund DustPhotonics. Future acquisitions may require additional equity dilution.

Growth Catalysts

  • Q2 FY2027 earnings (Aug 2026): First confirmation of H2 optical ramp trajectory. Guidance above $510M would signal H2 inflection building.
  • Hyperscaler AI capex announcements (Jul 2026): AWS, Azure, Google Q2 2026 earnings — any capex raise directly implies higher CRDO demand.
  • 200G per lane AEC volume ramp: CEO commentary indicates 200G customer engagement is growing. First volume production win at a hyperscaler is a major re-rating event.
  • DustPhotonics integration milestones: First public presentation of the integrated optical roadmap (ZeroFlap + DustPhotonics PICs) at an investor event would confirm the vertical integration thesis.
  • Sixth hyperscaler customer: International hyperscaler or sovereign AI infrastructure program win would confirm the TAM expansion story beyond US big-5.

The Central Debate & Investment Stance

Stance: CONSTRUCTIVE. The bull case for Credo is anchored in a genuine technical advantage (AEC reliability), exceptional execution track record (4 consecutive large beats), and a credible optical growth platform (ZeroFlap + DustPhotonics). PEG of 0.7x on 80%+ guided growth is inexpensive on a growth-adjusted basis relative to comparable AI semiconductor companies.

The central debate: Does the H2 FY2027 optical inflection materialise on the timeline management has guided — adding ~$600M+ of incremental annualised optical revenue by Q4 FY2027? If yes, the stock re-rates materially from $197. If the optical ramp slips to FY2028 due to DustPhotonics integration challenges or ZeroFlap volume qualification delays, the stock has limited upside on AEC alone at current levels.
